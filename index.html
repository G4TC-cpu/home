What stops people — and what is actually true

The things people tell themselves
that are not true.

The story

"I need to already be established and profitable to qualify for funding."

What is actually true

Funders fund documented readiness — not history. A clear financial picture, a real plan, and the right paperwork matter more than years in business. That is exactly what the Fundability Snapshot builds.

The story

"My idea is too small. This is for big organizations with staff and lawyers."

What is actually true

Wicomico County funded a parks department, a legal aid group, Habitat for Humanity, and a recovery resource center with CRRF money. Small, specific, and community-rooted is the whole point of these funds.

The story

"The compliance piece is too complicated. I will get it wrong and lose my license."

What is actually true

The MCA milestone process is nine steps over 24 months. Each step has a clear list of what you need and what your books need to show. My role is to walk you through all nine so you never have to guess what comes next.

The story

"Someone with more experience or bigger credentials will do this better than me."

What is actually true

The most useful advisor knows the terrain, speaks plainly, and shows up with receipts. You bring what no credential can give anyone else — your community, your presence, and your purpose.
